Bio

About

Nevi diSangue is a seasoned actor and voice talent with over fifteen years experience performing in theater, film, and character voicing. She majored in theater during her college days at Southwest Missouri State University, where she studied under visiting alumni such as Kathleen Turner, before moving to Chicago to expand and hone her technique in more professional settings as a student at Chicago Actor Studio and Second City. During her years in Illinois, she learned from the greats and was afforded opportunities to work with guest instructors like Wayne Brady of "Who's Line is it Anyway?"

Nevi's work ranges from full-length novel narrations to theater to independent short films to character voicing animations for student portfolios. In her early late-twenties, she decided acting was a better fit as a hobby rather than a profession and left the industry to focus on raising her children. After years as an at-home-mother, her youngest now in high school, she is returning to the acting scene with upcoming roles both on stage and in film and has landed her first voice actor position for an upcoming anime, titled Champion of Death (scheduled release TBD).
